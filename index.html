The change in climate conditions in Major Australian Cities

A data-driven exploration of temperature, rainfall, emissions and atmospheric trends across capital cities.

Created by R. Ramkumar - FIT2179 DataVis II (2026 Sem 1)

Climate Overview

This map illustrates the climatic diversity across Australia's capital cities. The size of each bubble represents total annual rainfall, while the color indicates average maximum temperatures.

A clear trend is visible where northern cities like Darwin exhibit both high temperatures and significant rainfall. Conversely, southern cities like Hobart show cooler, more temperate conditions.

This visualisation highlights the stark contrast in climate conditions across the continent, influenced by geographic location and proximity to the ocean. The tooltip provides detailed insights into each city's specific weather patterns, allowing for a deeper understanding of the regional climate variations.

Regional Climate Analysis

This section provides a deeper look into the evolving climate patterns across Australia. By examining the relationships between various weather metrics, we can observe long-term shifts that impact our daily lives and environment. Use this space to connect the trends seen in the map above with the specific data breakdowns provided in the charts below.

Rainfall observations

This line chart tracks the annual rainfall trends across Australia from 1910 to 2023. The interactive nature of the chart allows users to explore specific years and observe how rainfall patterns have evolved, revealing periods of drought and increased precipitation.

Sectoral Emissions Analysis

This stacked area chart visualizes the contribution of various sectors to Australia's total greenhouse gas emissions. By breaking down the data, we can identify which industries, such as energy or transport, are the primary drivers of emission trends over the years.

CO2 Emissions by Fuel

This interactive donut chart breaks down Australia's energy-related CO2 emissions by primary fuel source. It highlights the shifting proportions of Coal, Oil, and Natural Gas over the decades, providing insight into the transition of the national energy mix.

Temperature Anomalies

This heatmap visualises national annual temperature anomalies from 1910 to 2023. By grouping years into decades, we can easily identify historical warming trends, with red hues representing years significantly warmer than the average.
